What is PACO and what can it do?

Paco started as a way to scratch an itch. I wanted to understand how I was spending my time and how I could use it better. I wanted a personal assistant that I could tell what to manage and it would give me gentle nudges when I was straying from my plan.

It is named after our dog, Paco, who was a great personal assistant. He instinctively knew how I felt. When I was happy, he would bring the ball to play. When I was sad or tired he would nuzzle up to comfort me.

This sophisticated vision is still aspirational, not yet fully realized. Along the way, we have created a system that allows individuals and researchers to observe and experiment with behavior. Paco provides a platform with building blocks for behavioral theories. With it, you can sample the world, reason about it, and even develop interventions aimed at changing behavior. This is no small undertaking, and may never be fully accomplished, but by making it open source, working with researchers, and creating a place where participants and researchers can find each other, we hope we will make better progress and move our understanding of our behavior forward and maybe get to live better, healthier lives.

Get Involved!

Paco is open source because behavior is so complicated and there is so much yet to learn. It is more than anyone or any one team could ever hope to completely cover. We want to learn from each other while reducing the grunt work that each project requires prior to launching an experiment.

Paco is a platform for behavioral science to speed the implementation, testing and sharing of theories, frameworks, idioms, and tricks for understanding and changing behavior. Ultimately, we want to build in the language of behavior not Android, iOS, or Javascript. The problems are about humans not computers, web frameworks, data storage, security, signaling mechanisms, sensors, or a million other technical issues. So, let's share the grunt work and get to the good stuff.

We've made a good start but it's a tiny fraction of what we think will be needed. If you want to contribute to Paco, whether to fix a bug that is driving you crazy, add features to suit your research needs, or discuss the methodology and research, get involved with our developer site and our mailing list. We welcome contributions and enjoy working with other engineers and researchers excited about understanding behavior.
